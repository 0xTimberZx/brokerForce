# Glossary

## Core DeFi / LP Terms

**LP (Liquidity Provider / Liquidity Providing)** — A user who deposits assets into a pool to earn fees from trades, taking on price-relationship risk in exchange.

**Concentrated Liquidity** — An LP model (popularized by Uniswap v3) where a provider chooses a specific price range to supply liquidity in, rather than the full price curve. Earns more fees within that range, but earns nothing once price moves outside it.

**Range** — The price boundaries (min/max) an LP chooses for a concentrated liquidity position.

**In Range / Out of Range** — Whether the current price sits inside or outside an LP's chosen range. Out-of-range positions earn no fees until price returns or the position is rebalanced.

**Rebalance** — Adjusting or re-entering a position after price has moved outside its range.

**TVL (Total Value Locked)** — The total value of assets deposited in a pool or protocol.

**Impermanent Loss (IL)** — The value an LP "loses" relative to simply holding the assets, caused by price divergence between the two assets in a pair while liquidity is provided.

**APR / APY** — Annual Percentage Rate / Yield — commonly used (and, per `Vision.md`, distrusted as a standalone signal) to describe LP returns. BrokerForce treats these as a symptom to verify historically, not a number to trust at face value.

**Pool** — A specific on-chain liquidity venue for a pair (e.g. a particular Uniswap fee-tier pool for ETH/USDC on a specific chain). Multiple pools can exist for the same pair.

**Fee Tier** — The trading fee percentage a specific pool charges, which also determines the fee LPs earn.

**DEX (Decentralized Exchange)** — A protocol where trading happens via on-chain pools rather than a centralized order book (e.g. Uniswap, PancakeSwap, Aerodrome, Camelot).

## Statistical / Analytical Terms

**Correlation** — Pearson correlation coefficient between two assets' returns; how similarly they move.

**Beta** — Sensitivity of one asset's returns relative to another's.

**Cointegration** — A statistical measure of whether two assets' price relationship is stable over time, beyond simple correlation.

**Historical Volatility** — Standard deviation of an asset's (or pair's) returns over a given period.

**Relative Strength** — Performance of one asset versus another over time.

**Range Stability** — The historical percentage of time a pair's price relationship has stayed within a given band (e.g. ±2%, ±5%, ±10%, ±15%).

**Time in Range** — The average number of consecutive days a position would have stayed within a chosen range, historically.

**Fee Opportunity** — Estimated fees an LP position could have earned, based on volume, TVL, and range width.

**Backtest** — Simulating a strategy (e.g. an LP range) against historical data to see how it would have actually performed.

## BrokerForce-Specific Terms

**ORT (Opportunity Risk/Time)** — BrokerForce's composite 0–100 score combining seven weighted inputs (Range Stability, Time in Range, Correlation, Liquidity, Volume, Volatility, Market Cap Stability) into one comparable measure of pair quality. See `ORT.md`.

**Canonical Window** — One of three fixed periods (30d / 90d / 200d) ORT is computed and stored on, kept independent rather than blended, so scores stay comparable across pairs. 90d is the default shown.

**Quadrant Label** — ORT's qualitative read of a pair, placing it on a Volume × Volatility grid (**Prime**, **Active**, **Quiet**, **Avoid** — names are a first pass, not final) plus a trend indicator showing whether it's moving toward or away from Prime.

**Pair** — BrokerForce's core unit of analysis: two assets treated as a single relationship object with its own statistical profile, generated by the Pair Engine.

**Pair Engine** — The system that generates every relevant asset-pair combination and computes their statistical profiles; tiers pairs into full-depth (active/popular) vs. limited analysis (less active combinations), and excludes stable–stable pairs from full treatment.

**Volume Field Set** — The pair-level derived volume metrics (`avg_volume_24h`, `avg_volume_7d`, `avg_volume_30d`, `volume_tvl_ratio`, `volume_trend`, `volume_stability`, `volume_share`, `fee_opportunity_score`) used as first-class Pair Engine inputs, not just display data.

**Asset Class Tiers** — BrokerForce's classification of tracked assets: **Blue chip** (BTC, ETH, BNB, SOL), **Stable** (USDC, USDT, FRAX, SKY, DAI), **Growth/Exotic** (ONDO, NEAR, LINK, AAVE, AVAX, ARB), **Degen** (PEPE, BONK, WIF, FLOKI, MEME).

**The Four Pillars** — Discover, Analyze, Optimize, Monitor — the four functions that define the product itself, independent of any specific page or feature.

**The DNA Check** — The five-question test (see `Product_Principles.md`) every feature is evaluated against before being built.

**Protocol Agnostic** — Built so the analytics engine works the same way regardless of which DEX, chain, or protocol the underlying data comes from — a core architectural commitment, not a marketing claim.
